The work

Three things I built that didn't exist before I showed up.

01
Growth Strategy
The Phoenix News · 2024
From invisible to unmissable — growing a student publication's audience by 70%
The problem

The Phoenix News had a social media presence that looked like an afterthought — sporadic posts, generic campus-news content, no personality. Engagement was flat and follower growth had stalled. Nobody was paying attention.

What I did

I scrapped the existing approach and rebuilt the content strategy from scratch. Instead of pushing every article, I created content formats students actually engaged with: opinion polls, behind-the-scenes newsroom content, trending audio reels, and fast-turnaround reactions to campus events. I tracked performance weekly, killed what wasn't working, and doubled down on what was. Within weeks, the data was clear — personality and speed beat polish and volume every time.

02
Program Design · Community
UBC Okanagan · 2024–Present
Building a mentorship program that actually worked — 135 matches and counting
The problem

First-year students at UBCO were struggling to find their footing — overwhelmed by a new city, a new institution, and no real roadmap. The existing peer support structures were sparse and transactional. Students needed genuine connection with people who'd already figured it out.

What I did

I designed and ran a mentorship matching program from the ground up. Built the application and matching process, trained upper-year mentors on how to actually be useful (not just available), planned orientation events that brought people together before the stress set in, and ran the communication strategy that kept participants engaged beyond week one. The goal wasn't just matches — it was relationships that lasted the year.

03
Editorial Strategy · Product
The Phoenix News · 2026
Relaunching a student newsroom — new website, first magazine, zero budget
The problem

When I became Editor-in-Chief, The Phoenix News had an outdated website that didn't reflect the quality of the journalism, no print presence, and an editorial process that ran on hope. The publication needed to look and operate like a real newsroom — not a student club.

What I did

I rebuilt the website from scratch, restructured the editorial calendar so deadlines were actually met, and launched the publication's first-ever print magazine — coordinating design, editorial, and production simultaneously. Every decision was made without a budget and with a team of student volunteers. The result was a publication that finally looked as credible as the stories it was telling.
