Design · Community
KCR Community Resources · 2024
Turning life stories into something families keep forever
The context

KCR Community Resources runs programs for families navigating difficult circumstances — foster care, trauma, transition. The Lifebook program helps children and youth process their histories through a personalized memory book. The design needed to feel warm, accessible, and meaningful — not clinical.

What I did

I designed Lifebooks that translated each child's unique story into a cohesive, visually engaging keepsake. That meant collaborating with caseworkers to understand each person's background, making deliberate layout and visual choices to reflect personality, and producing something that could be handed to a family and understood immediately — no explanation needed.

The Phoenix News · 2026
Relaunching a student newsroom — new site, first magazine, zero budget
The context

When I became Editor-in-Chief, The Phoenix News had an outdated website, no print presence, and an editorial process that ran on goodwill. The publication needed to operate like a real newsroom — not a student club.

What I did

I rebuilt the website from scratch, restructured the editorial calendar so deadlines were actually met, and launched the publication's first-ever print magazine — coordinating design, editorial, and production simultaneously. I also grew the social media audience by 70% as Social Media Manager before taking the top role — so I understood the publication inside out before I ever had the title.

Program Design · Community
UBC Okanagan Res Life · 2025
Building a residence community that actually felt like one
The context

Living in residence is supposed to be a formative experience — but without intentional programming, it defaults to isolation. As Initiatives Coordinator, I was responsible for building the events, structures, and touchpoints that made a floor of strangers feel like a community.

What I did

I designed and ran programming that met students where they were — not generic icebreakers, but events tailored to the specific mix of people on the floor, timed to the points in the year when students most needed connection (move-in week, midterms, exam season). I coordinated with Residence Life staff, managed logistics, and made sure every event had a clear purpose beyond "something to do."
